About Thyroid Cancer

Thyroid cancer is a type of cancer that originates from the thyroid gland, a butterfly-shaped gland located at the base of your neck. It is relatively rare compared to other types of cancer but can be serious if not treated early.

Common symptoms of thyroid cancer include a lump or swelling in the neck, difficulty swallowing, persistent hoarseness, and changes in voice.

Early detection and treatment of thyroid cancer can greatly improve outcomes and reduce the risk of complications.

Prevention

While the exact cause of thyroid cancer is not known, there are steps you can take to reduce your risk:

  • Regularly check your neck for any lumps or swelling.
  • Avoid exposure to radiation whenever possible.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Limit your intake of processed foods and sugary drinks.
  • Exercise regularly and maintain a healthy weight.
